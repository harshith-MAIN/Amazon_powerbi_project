The primary goal of this analysis is to visualize sales velocity, product performance, and media catalog distribution. It bridges the gap between e-commerce metrics and digital content metadata, allowing for a holistic understanding of brand engagement.

Visualization: Power BI Desktop
Data Modeling: DAX (Total YTD/QTD Sales, Review Summations)
ETL: Power Query for data cleaning and joining datasets.
Design: Modern Dark-Theme UI with intuitive navigation between Data and Prime views.Data Sources: Amazon Sales, Amazon Prime Movie dataset
Data Transformation: Power Query (DAX for custom measures and calculated columns)

Dash board Analysis
1. Amazon Retail Analysis (E-commerce)

    Performance Metrics: Tracks Total YTD Sales (2M), Total QTD Sales (811K), and Total Reviews (19M).
    Temporal Trends: Monthly Sales: Area chart showing YTD sales velocity by month.
    Weekly Sales: Column chart analyzing sales by week number to identify high-traffic periods.
    Product Insights: * Top products ranked by YTD Sales 
    Interactive Slicers: Filter by Product Category, Shipment Method, and a custom Date Range.

2. Amazon Prime Video Analysis (Streaming)
    Content Inventory: Summarizes a library of over 6,000 Movies and 30M+ total ratings/reviews.
    Release Trends: Line chart visualizing the count of product descriptions by Release Year, highlighting the massive growth in content since 2000.
    Content Composition: * Movie Type Distribution: Pie chart breaking down the catalog by ratings (CC, R, PG-13, TV-MA, etc. 
    Talent Filters: Dedicated dropdowns for Director, Actor, and Movie Type.

Key Insights Captured

  Sales Seasonality: Significant sales spikes visible in the latter half of the year (Weeks 40–50), likely correlating with holiday shopping.
  Content Strategy: A high concentration of "CC" and "R" rated content dominates the Prime library.
  Market Leaders: Identification of tech brands (Nikon, SanDisk) as primary drivers of both revenue and customer engagement.
